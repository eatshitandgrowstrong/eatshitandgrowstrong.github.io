Traffic Light example

This is an example of a traffic light for motorists using the arduino and some LEDs.

Below is the source code:

I first start by configuring my input and output pins, in this case, using the integrated INPUT_PULLUP resistor for my input.

By default, the traffic light for motorists is green, with the other yellow and red LEDs at the LOW status.

When the push button is pressed by a pedestrian, due to the switch being default 'pulled HIGH' by the INPUT_PULLUP resistor, the push button is at an active LOW state when it is pushed.

The push button therefore sends a LOW signal to the input.

When the input LOW is received, the microcontroller delays and waits for 5 seconds before turning on the yellow LED to indicate to motorists to slow down and switches off the green LED.

After another 4 seconds, the yellow LED is turned off and the red LED is turned on for 10 seconds.

With the help of a 'for loop' I am able to have a 'flasher' LED flash on and off for half a second after 5 seconds to indicate to pedestrians that the traffic is turning green soon.
